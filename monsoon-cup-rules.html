Suncity Monsoon Cup 2025

Official Rules Draft

Important Note:

Rules for 2s and 4s will be explained to all team owners before the start of the tournament or their respective matches.

1. Umpire's Decision and Authority

  • The umpire's decision is final and binding.
  • The umpires can consult with each other to make a final decision, especially in doubtful situations.
  • Caught Behind: Only the main umpire has the authority to make a caught behind decision. The leg umpire can be consulted by the main umpire only in case of a doubtful catch.
  • Leg Umpire's Role: The leg umpire will only take calls on waist-high no-balls, run-outs, and stumpings.

2. Captains' Role & On-field Conduct

  • Only the team captain is allowed to communicate with the umpire regarding any rule discussions.
  • If any other players enter the field to discuss a decision, a penalty may be imposed on their team.

3. Playing XI

  • Teams must declare their playing XI before the match.
  • Out of the declared XI, only 10 players can field at any given time.
  • Any 10 players from the XI can bat or bowl.
  • Minimum Players: A minimum of 7 players is required to play a match. A maximum of 2 fielding substitutes may be used, but only if the team does not have 10 players on the field. Fielding substitutes must be agreed upon by the umpire and the opposition captain.

4. Overs & Bowling Restrictions

  • League matches will be 10 overs per side.
  • A maximum of 3 overs can be bowled by one bowler; all other bowlers can bowl a maximum of 2 overs.

5. On-field Official

  • An official will be announced before each match and will be responsible for all calls on balls going over or through the straight boundary grill.
  • This person's decision will be final in these situations to avoid any on-field controversies.

6. International Rules

  • All international cricket rules will apply except for LBW and leg byes.

7. Leg-Side Boundary Rules

  • If the ball goes out over the leg-side boundary without touching anything or touches only the wire fencing, it will be considered out.
  • If the ball bounces on top of the boundary wall and then goes out, it will be not out, and 2 runs will be awarded.
  • The same rules apply to the back-side boundary wall.

8. Straight Shots and Trees

  • A straight shot that touches any part of the trees and falls out of the ground will be considered out.
  • Tree Trunk Rule: The tree at the corner of the long-off boundary, which divides the off-side 2 and 6 platform, will have special rules: if the ball hits the trunk of that tree, it will not be declared a six or a two.

9. Grill on Straight Boundaries

  • If the ball passes through the grill directly, it will be a 6.
  • If the ball goes over the grill without touching the benches or ground, it will be considered out.

10. Team Equipment

  • Each team must bring their own bats and accessories.
  • Balls and stumps will be provided by the organizers.
  • Plastic and synthetic bats are not allowed.

11. Additional Rules

  • Last Man Batting: The last man batting will not be allowed under any condition.
